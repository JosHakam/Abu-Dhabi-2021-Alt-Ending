EXPLAINED:
How Lewis Hamilton won one of the most chaotic, controversial title showdowns in F1 history.

By Chris Medland

Formula 1 Abu Dhabi Lewis Hamilton Max Verstappen

In some ways, it shouldn't have been a surprise that a remarkable see-sawing title fight was settled by a remarkable see–sawing finale. After 1,296 laps, it all came down to the 1,297th and final. For two hours, Lewis Hamilton was on course for an unparalleled eighth title. But one lap from the end, one lap from history, it all swung – and it was Max Verstappen with the advantage.

Make no mistake, Lewis deserved this title. But so too did Verstappen. Both men drove sublimely all year, their level never dipping below phenomenal. But one man had to win, and one had to lose. Why, then, was the manner in which that happened so controversial? Why did it produce such ill–feeling and rancour that the stewards were involved? And where does the sport go from here?

As Hamilton kept repeating after clinching his eigth crown, only one word summed it up – 'insane'. We break down what happened on a night that will now forever be etched into F1 history...
